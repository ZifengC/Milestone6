# Recommender Systems

Recommender systems help users discover items such as products, movies, articles, or courses. They usually rank candidate items according to predicted user preference. Common approaches include collaborative filtering, content-based recommendation, hybrid models, and sequence-based recommendation.

Collaborative filtering uses patterns from user-item interactions. If two users have similar histories, items liked by one user may be recommended to the other. Content-based recommendation uses item attributes such as product category, text description, brand, topic, or price.

Modern recommender systems often include personalization signals. These can include explicit ratings, clicks, purchases, search queries, dwell time, and user profiles. The system may also use context such as time, device, location, or current session behavior.

Evaluation usually includes offline ranking metrics and online business metrics. Offline metrics include precision, recall, NDCG, and mean reciprocal rank. Online metrics may include click-through rate, conversion rate, revenue, user satisfaction, and long-term retention.

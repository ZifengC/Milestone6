# Personalization Bias

Personalization bias occurs when a system overuses prior user behavior or popularity signals and narrows what the user sees. In recommender systems, this can create filter bubbles where users repeatedly receive similar items and have fewer chances to discover diverse content.

Popularity bias is a common related issue. Popular items receive more exposure, which produces more interactions, which then makes them appear even more relevant. This feedback loop can make niche or new items difficult to surface even when they match a user's needs.

Personalization can also amplify social bias if recommendations are based heavily on group-level behavior. A system may infer preferences from similar users and reinforce stereotypes or majority patterns rather than the individual's current intent.

Mitigation strategies include diversity-aware ranking, exploration, fairness constraints, debiasing training data, and measuring exposure across item groups. A balanced recommender should remain relevant while still giving users opportunities to discover novel and less popular items.
